## Objective

This project started in March 2024 as a university survey assignment for the Environment faculty at South Asian University, New Delhi. The goal was simple: understand how people actually feel about Electric Vehicles, what stops them from adopting EVs, and whether they believe EVs genuinely help the environment.

Two years later, I revisited the data and expanded this into a full research and analysis project. A lot has changed since 2024 — the problems people feared back then have partially come true, some new ones have emerged, and the EV story in India has become far more complicated than the headlines suggest.

This repository contains everything: the original survey data and analysis, an updated research document on the state of EVs in India in 2026, a detailed data analysis file with trends and charts, and a summary of findings.

---

## What I Did

**Phase 1 — March 2024 (Original Survey)**
- Designed a mixed-format survey on Google Forms with both MCQ and open-ended questions
- Distributed it across different demographic groups — college students, working adults, and general public — both online and offline
- Collected 51 responses
- Performed frequency analysis and cross-tabulation to understand patterns in EV awareness, usage, and perception

**Phase 2 — June 2026 (Research & Updated Analysis)**
- Went back to the survey findings and compared them against what actually happened in India's EV sector between 2024 and 2026
- Researched real data from VAHAN Dashboard, EVreporter, JMK Research, IEA, WRI India, and government policy documents
- Updated the analysis to include the current state of EV infrastructure in Delhi, the Ola Electric controversy, battery degradation issues, charging infrastructure gaps, and what India's EV trajectory looks like heading toward 2030
- Wrote a research document covering the full timeline of EV adoption in India with references

**Total time invested:** Approximately 3 weeks across both phases (original survey + updated research and documentation)

## Key Findings (Quick Overview)

- In our 2024 survey, 70.6% of respondents had never used an EV. The top barrier was high cost (49%), followed by poor charging infrastructure (45.1%) and long charging time (43.1%). These concerns turned out to be accurate.
- Between 2024 and 2026, India's EV sales grew from ~1.95 million to over 2.7 million units annually — but the technology and service quality did not keep pace with sales volume.
- Ola Electric, once the market leader in e-scooters, received over 10,000 government-level complaints between 2023 and 2024 alone — validating what many survey respondents suspected about technology reliability.
- Delhi currently has 8,849 charging points against a requirement of 36,150. The gap is not a future problem — it is a present crisis.
- EVs in India save approximately 20% fewer lifecycle CO2 emissions than ICE vehicles today (IEA, 2024), largely because India's grid still relies heavily on coal. This number will improve as renewable energy grows.
- 47.1% of our survey respondents said EVs will replace 70–80% of conventional vehicles. Based on India's 2030 targets and current trajectory, that estimate is plausible but ambitious.

## Note

This project is honest. The survey was conducted in March 2024 with 51 respondents from a limited demographic, primarily urban youth in Delhi. The findings should not be generalised to rural India or older demographics. The 2026 analysis draws from publicly available data and is cross-referenced with multiple sources. Where data was uncertain, it has been noted.
